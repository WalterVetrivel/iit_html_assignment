Salvation

What must I do to be saved?

We have all sinned against God

God, as our Creator, commands us how to live. But we have rebelled against him and broken his commandments. Even "good" people have fallen short of God's commandments. His standard is so high that he says even looking at a woman with lust is adultery and getting angry without reason is equivalent to murder.

"As it is written, There is none righteous, no, not one: There is none that understandeth, there is none that seeketh after God. They are all gone out of the way, they are together become unprofitable; there is none that doeth good, no, not one." Romans 3:10-12
"For all have sinned, and come short of the glory of God;" Romans 3:23

We are deserving of God's judgment

As we have sinned against God, we are all under condemnation. As we've seen before, God is our judge, and he is a righteous judge, therefore he will rightfully punish us for our rebellion against him. The wages of sin is death.

"For the wages of sin is death." Romans 6:23
"And as it is appointed unto men once to die, but after this the judgment:" Hebrews 9:27
"But unto them that are contentious, and do not obey the truth, but obey unrighteousness, indignation and wrath, Tribulation and anguish, upon every soul of man that doeth evil, of the Jew first, and also of the Gentile;" Romans 2:8-9

Jesus comes as our Saviour

Just as God is righteous and just, he is also loving and merciful. Our sin demands that a price be paid. At the same time, God, in his mercy, desires to save us from our sins and our judgment. Therefore, God sent his only begotten Son Jesus Christ, who is equal to and one with God, into this world as a man to deliver us from our sins.

"And we have seen and do testify that the Father sent the Son to be the Saviour of the world." 1 John 4:14

Jesus was born of a virgin, i.e., he didn't have a biological human Father, he was miraculously conceived by the power of the Holy Spirit in fulfillment of a prophecy by the prophet Isaiah, and he lived a perfect and sinless life in obedience to God the Father.

"And she shall bring forth a son, and thou shalt call his name Jesus: for he shall save his people from their sins. Now all this was done, that it might be fulfilled which was spoken of the Lord by the prophet, saying, Behold, a virgin shall be with child, and shall bring forth a son, and they shall call his name Emmanuel, which being interpreted is, God with us." Matthew 1:21-23
"For we have not an high priest which cannot be touched with the feeling of our infirmities; but was in all points tempted like as we are, yet without sin." Hebrews 4:15

The death and resurrection of Christ

As Jesus Christ lived a perfect and sinless life, he alone was worthy to pay for all our sins. He is identified as the Lamb of God that takes away the sins of the world. After living a sinless and holy life, when he was about 33 years old, he was put to death on a cross, where he paid for our sins.

"But he was wounded for our transgressions, he was bruised for our iniquities: the chastisement of our peace was upon him; and with his stripes we are healed. All we like sheep have gone astray; we have turned every one to his own way; and the Lord hath laid on him the iniquity of us all." Isaiah 53:5-6
"Even as the Son of man came not to be ministered unto, but to minister, and to give his life a ransom for many." Matthew 20:28
"For I delivered unto you first of all that which I also received, how that Christ died for our sins according to the scriptures;" 1 Corinthians 15:3

Having died for our sins, Jesus did not stay dead, and as he won the victory over sin on the cross, he also defeated death by rising from the dead.

"He is not here: for he is risen, as he said. Come, see the place where the Lord lay." Matthew 28:6
"Knowing that Christ being raised from the dead dieth no more; death hath no more dominion over him." Romans 6:9
"And that he was buried, and that he rose again the third day according to the scriptures:" 1 Corinthians 15:4

Repent and believe the gospel

This message of God reconciling us to himself through the death and resurrection of his Son is what we call the gospel or good news. In order to receive forgiveness of sins and eternal life through Christ, we are called to repent, to confess our sins and to turn from our evil ways, and to believe, to put our faith and trust in the Lord Jesus Christ.

"And saying, The time is fulfilled, and the kingdom of God is at hand: repent ye, and believe the gospel." Mark 1:15

You have heard the good news, the gospel of your salvation. Having heard, now repent and believe, for it is written,

"That if thou shalt confess with thy mouth the Lord Jesus, and shalt believe in thine heart that God hath raised him from the dead, thou shalt be saved." Romans 10:9

Therefore, I urge you to repent and believe the gospel today, calling on the name of Jesus Christ. Nobody is guaranteed another day. Today is the day of salvation. Today if you hear his voice, harden not your heart. We are saved by grace through faith, not our own works. God has given you grace by sending his Son to pay for your sins. Now, receive that grace through faith in his name.

New identity, baptism and the Holy Spirit

When we truly repent and place our faith in Jesus Christ, God gives us new life. He cleanses us from our sins through the blood of Jesus. He removes our hearts of stone and gives us hearts of flesh, and puts his Holy Spirit inside us to teach us and guide us and empower us to live holy lives.

"Therefore if any man be in Christ, he is a new creature: old things are passed away; behold, all things are become new." 2 Corinthians 5:17
"Then will I sprinkle clean water upon you, and ye shall be clean: from all your filthiness, and from all your idols, will I cleanse you. A new heart also will I give you, and a new spirit will I put within you: and I will take away the stony heart out of your flesh, and I will give you an heart of flesh. And I will put my spirit within you, and cause you to walk in my statutes, and ye shall keep my judgments, and do them." Ezekiel 36:25-27

We publicly proclaim that we have died to our old self and have become new creatures by being baptised in water, which shows that we have been buried with Christ in baptism and as Christ was raised up, we are also raised to new life in him.

"How shall we, that are dead to sin, live any longer therein? Know ye not, that so many of us as were baptized into Jesus Christ were baptized into his death? Therefore we are buried with him by baptism into death: that like as Christ was raised up from the dead by the glory of the Father, even so we also should walk in newness of life." Romans 6:2b-4

Summary

To summarise,

  1. All have sinned and come short of the glory of God
  2. The wages of sin is death
  3. Jesus came to save us from our sins
  4. He died for our sins and rose again on the third day
  5. To receive the free gift of eternal life, we need to repent and trust in Christ
  6. When we repent and place our faith in him, God gives us his Holy Spirit and we are made new creatures in Christ
  7. In obedience to God, we get baptised signifying that our old self is dead and buried with Christ, and that we are raised into new life in Christ
